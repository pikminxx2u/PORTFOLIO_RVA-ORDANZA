Artist Statement

In my own words

Visual art has always been a language I wanted to become fluent in, but honestly I'm still learning how to speak it. This portfolio shows that process, not just the finished works, but how my way of seeing changed over time.

At first, I used to just look at images for what they show. Now I notice more things, like why certain colors feel strong or why empty space sometimes says more than details. I still get confused sometimes when making decisions, like what color to use or how to arrange elements, but I think that's part of it.

As an IT student, I didn't expect to enjoy the creative side this much. But I realized design and coding are kind of similar, both need structure, but also some creativity to actually work well.

This isn't a perfect collection, and some parts could be better, but it shows real progress. And I think that matters more.

About

Reading the visual world

This portfolio shows my journey in Reading Visual Art. It explores how images communicate meaning using the elements and principles of art across eleven modules.

Each work is not just shown, but also understood as a visual language, using line, shape, color, texture, space, and composition to express ideas and emotion.

Color Theory Line & Form Composition Texture Value Space Symbolism Art Analysis Photography Digital Design Pattern Design Visual Rhetoric

Skills explored: Color Theory · Line & Form · Composition · Texture · Value · Space · Symbolism · Art Analysis · Photography · Digital Design · Pattern Design · Visual Rhetoric

My approach focuses on understanding both what an artwork shows and how it communicates meaning. This collection includes Modules 1 to 11, each highlighting a different concept learned in the course, with a focus on how visual design connects with technology and communication.
